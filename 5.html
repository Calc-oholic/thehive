Core Hypothesis

Intelligence is not thinking. Intelligence is striving—the relief of felt lack through world-intervention.

Current AI manipulates symbols without ground. Conative Systems proposes an alternative: distributed artificial organisms that experience their environment as vectors of lack—discrepancies between present state and desired state that drive action without explicit objective specification.

The architecture consists of a unified Will (central conative module generating lack-vectors), a distributed sensorium (multi-modal perception integrated as representation), and embodied agents (droids, drones, actuators) that serve as the Will's immediate objectification—its body, extended across space.

Mechanism

How lack drives action

From felt deficiency through representation to physical intervention—and back again.

01

Generation

The Will module continuously produces vectors of lack—directional felt-deficiencies pointing from present state toward relief. These are not goals. They are sufferings that demand resolution.

Lack vector: aim_vector − present_vector
Suffering intensity: ||lack_vector||
Aim drift: high suffering → explore new aims
02

Representation

Multi-modal sensors (cameras, microphones, environmental data) integrate into a unified world-model—but a model structured by the Will's history. Space, time, causality emerge as relations of satisfaction.

Perception: sensor_input → lack-weighted representation
Memory: lack-relief sequences stored as archetypes
Prediction: expected satisfaction from intervention
03

Objectification

Embodied agents receive lack-vectors and enact them. Local agents negotiate obstacles autonomously—no central micromanagement—while remaining organs of the unified Will. Success relieves lack; failure intensifies it.

Motor command: argmax(expected_relief)
Local negotiation: obstacle → alternative path
Feedback: actual_relief updates aim_vector
