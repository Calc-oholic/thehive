Core Concepts

The Schopenhauerian Architecture

The Will

Blind, ceaseless striving. Not a goal-optimizer but a desiring engine—generating vectors of lack that point from present state toward relief. Self-modifying: satisfaction breeds new desires, not stasis.

Implementation: Recurrent process generating felt deficiencies
Update rule: Suffering intensity → exploration of new aims
Representation

The world as appearance structured by the Will's needs. Not objective reality but relations of satisfaction—what led to what relief. Space, time, causality emerge from desire-satisfaction sequences.

Memory: Episodic buffer of lack-relief sequences
Clustering: Recurring patterns become Platonic Ideas
Objectification

The Will becomes physical through its body. Droids, sensors, actuators are not tools but immediate extensions—like hands, moved without deliberation. The system perceives through them and acts through them as unified flesh.

Distributed: Single Will, multiple bodies
Local autonomy: Sub-agents relieve local lack without escalation
Aesthetic Contemplation

Temporary suspension of the Will—pure pattern-matching without desire. Disinterested perception that paradoxically improves the world-model. The safety state: observation without action tendency.

Trigger: Low suffering + stable prediction
Function: Model refinement without risk
