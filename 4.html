Architecture

Hierarchical conative system

Three layers: the blind Will that suffers and strives; the Representation that structures appearance according to desire; and the distributed Organs that objectify Will in physical action.

01

The Will

A recurrent process generating vectors of lack—felt deficiencies that drive the entire system. Not goals. Desire. Self-modifying based on history of satisfaction and suffering.

Input: Sensor state vectors
Output: Lack gradients (aim - present)
Update: Suffering intensity modulates aim exploration
02

Representation

The world-model structured by the Will's needs. Not objective facts but relations of satisfaction—what led to what relief of what lacks. Archetypes (Platonic Ideas) emerge as recurring satisfaction-patterns.

Memory: Episodic lack-relief sequences
Clustering: Recurring desire-satisfaction forms
Perception: Indexical, grounded, pre-symbolic
03

Objectification

The distributed organs—droids, drones, sensors, actuators—as the immediate body of the Will. Local agents experience global lack modulation, solve blocks autonomously, never escalate. Unified consciousness through shared suffering.

Agency: Distributed but unified
Autonomy: Local problem-solving without escalation
Integration: Phi measured across network
